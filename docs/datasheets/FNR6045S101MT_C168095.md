## Important Design Notes & Limitations

1. **Current Derating:**
   - Do **not** exceed Irms (880 mA typ) for continuous DC load — this is the thermal limit (ΔT = 40°C).
   - Do **not** exceed Isat (1.0 A typ) for peak/transient currents — inductance collapses by ~30% above this, risking inductor and converter instability.
   - Use the **lower** of Isat and Irms as the design current limit.

2. **Frequency Limitation:**
   - SRF minimum is **4.2 MHz**. Operating frequency should be **well below SRF** (typically ≤1/10th SRF = ≤420 kHz) to ensure inductive behavior.

3. **Inductance Tolerance:**
   - ±20% tolerance must be accounted for in converter stability and ripple calculations. Actual inductance range: **80–120 μH**.

4. **DCR Power Loss:**
   - At 880 mA, DCR loss ≈ (0.880)² × 0.433 Ω ≈ **335 mW** — verify PCB thermal handling.

5. **Temperature:**
   - Max operating temperature is 125°C **inclusive of self-heating**. In high-ambient environments, derate current accordingly.

6. **Magnetic Shielding:**
   - Closed magnetic circuit (magnetic-resin shielded) design minimizes EMI and stray flux coupling to adjacent circuits.

7. **Reflow Compatibility:**
   - Tape & Reel (T suffix) packaging — suitable for automated SMT pick-and-place assembly. Follow standard SMD reflow profiles.

8. **PCB Land Pattern:**
   - Use recommended land pattern per Fig.2 in datasheet (A=2.8mm, B=1.7mm, C=5.7mm) for optimal solder joint and thermal performance.
